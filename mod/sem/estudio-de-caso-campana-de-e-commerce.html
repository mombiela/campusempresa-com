Case Study: E-commerce Campaign
Estudio de caso: Campaña de generación de leads ►

Introduction

In this case study, we will analyze a search engine marketing (SEM) campaign for an e-commerce site. We will explore from the initial setup to advanced optimization, providing practical examples and exercises to reinforce learning.

Initial Campaign Setup

Keyword Research

  • Research Tools: Google Keyword Planner, SEMrush, Ahrefs.
  • Types of Keywords:
    • Short-tail keywords: High competition, high search volume.
    • Long-tail keywords: Lower competition, more specific.
Example:
- Short-tail: "shoes"
- Long-tail: "men's leather shoes"

Ad Creation

  • Ad Elements:
    • Title: Attracts the user's attention.
    • Description: Explains the product's value.
    • Visible URL: Should be clear and relevant.
Ad Example:
Title: Buy Men's Leather Shoes - Free Shipping
Description: Discover our collection of men's leather shoes. Guaranteed quality and free shipping on orders over $50.
Visible URL: www.example.com/mens-shoes

Campaign Setup in Google Ads

  • Campaign Structure:
    • Campaigns: Highest level, defines the overall goal.
    • Ad Groups: Groupings of ads and keywords.
    • Ads: Specific messages that will be shown to users.
Structure Example:
- Campaign: Leather Shoes
  - Ad Group: Men's Leather Shoes
    - Ad 1: Title and Description
    - Ad 2: Title and Description
  - Ad Group: Women's Leather Shoes
    - Ad 1: Title and Description
    - Ad 2: Title and Description

Campaign Optimization

Performance Monitoring and Analysis

  • Key Metrics:
    • CTR (Click-Through Rate): Percentage of clicks relative to impressions.
    • CPC (Cost Per Click): Average cost per click.
    • Conversions: Number of desired actions completed by users.
Metrics Example:
- CTR: 5%
- CPC: $0.50
- Conversions: 100

Bid Adjustments

  • Bidding Strategies:
    • Manual CPC: Full control over bids.
    • Enhanced CPC: Automatic adjustments to maximize conversions.
    • Target CPA: Optimization to achieve a specific cost per acquisition.
Adjustment Example:
- If CPC is high and conversions are low, consider manually reducing the bid or switching to enhanced CPC.

A/B Testing

  • Elements to Test:
    • Ad Titles: Different approaches to see which resonates more.
    • Descriptions: Variations in the message to improve conversion.
    • Landing Pages: Different pages to see which converts better.
A/B Test Example:
- Ad A: "Buy Men's Leather Shoes - Free Shipping"
- Ad B: "Men's Leather Shoes - 20% Off"

Advanced Optimization

Advanced Segmentation

  • Audience Segmentation:
    • Remarketing Audiences: Users who have already visited the site.
    • Similar Audiences: Users with behaviors similar to your current customers.

Use of Ad Extensions

  • Types of Extensions:
    • Sitelink Extensions: Additional links to different pages on the site.
    • Call Extensions: Phone number for direct contact.
    • Location Extensions: Physical address of the business.
Extension Example:
- Sitelink Extension: "New Arrivals", "Special Offers", "Women's Shoes", "Men's Shoes"

Conclusion

In this case study, we have covered the fundamental and advanced aspects of creating and optimizing an SEM campaign for an e-commerce site. From keyword research to advanced segmentation and the use of ad extensions, each step is crucial to maximizing performance and achieving marketing goals. Practicing with real examples and making continuous adjustments based on data analysis is key to success in SEM.

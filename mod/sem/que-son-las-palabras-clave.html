What are Keywords?
Herramientas para la investigación de palabras clave ►

Introduction

Keywords are terms or phrases that users enter into search engines to find information, products, or services. In the context of Search Engine Marketing (SEM), keywords are fundamental for creating effective advertising campaigns and attracting relevant traffic to a website.

Importance of Keywords in SEM

  • Relevance: Keywords help connect ads with users' search queries.
  • Targeting: They allow ads to be directed to a specific audience.
  • Cost: The cost of SEM campaigns can vary depending on the competition of the selected keywords.
  • Performance: Choosing the right keywords can improve the performance of advertising campaigns.

Types of Keywords

Exact Match Keywords

  • Definition: Exactly match the user's search query.
  • Example: If the keyword is [red shoes], the ad will only show when someone searches exactly "red shoes".

Phrase Match Keywords

  • Definition: Include the exact phrase but can have additional words before or after.
  • Example: If the keyword is "red shoes", the ad will show for searches like "buy red shoes" or "cheap red shoes".

Broad Match Keywords

  • Definition: Allow the ad to show for related searches, even if they do not contain the exact words.
  • Example: If the keyword is red shoes, the ad might show for searches like "red footwear" or "shoes in red color".

Negative Keywords

  • Definition: Exclude certain words to prevent the ad from showing in irrelevant searches.
  • Example: If the negative keyword is free, the ad will not show for searches that include "free red shoes".

Keyword Selection

Keyword Research

  • Tools: Use tools like Google Keyword Planner, SEMrush, or Ahrefs to find relevant keywords.
  • Search Volume: Consider the number of monthly searches for each keyword.
  • Competition: Evaluate the competition for each keyword and its cost per click (CPC).

Selection Strategies

  • Long Tail Keywords: Longer and more specific keywords that usually have less competition and a lower cost per click.
  • Short Tail Keywords: Shorter and more general keywords that usually have more competition and a higher cost per click.

Practical Example

Suppose you have an online store that sells sports shoes. Here is an example of how you could select and organize your keywords:

| Keyword Type | Keyword Example | Description | |-----------------------------|---------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------| | Exact Match | [sports shoes] | Ad only shows for "sports shoes" | | Phrase Match | "sports shoes" | Ad shows for "buy sports shoes" | | Broad Match | sports shoes | Ad shows for "athletic footwear" | | Negative Keyword | free | Ad does not show for "free sports shoes" |

Conclusion

Keywords are an essential component of SEM, as they determine when and where your ads will be shown. Understanding the different types of keywords and how to select them appropriately can significantly improve the performance of your advertising campaigns. Through careful research and a well-planned strategy, you can attract relevant traffic and maximize your return on investment in advertising.

Introduction

In this section, you will learn how to set up a basic SEM (Search Engine Marketing) campaign. This process is fundamental for any digital marketing strategy, as it allows you to attract qualified traffic to your website through paid ads on search engines like Google.

Key Concepts

Before starting the setup, it is important to understand some basic concepts:

  • SEM (Search Engine Marketing): Digital marketing strategy that uses paid ads on search engines to increase the visibility of a website.
  • CPC (Cost per Click): Amount paid each time a user clicks on your ad.
  • CTR (Click-Through Rate): Percentage of users who click on your ad after seeing it.
  • Keywords: Terms or phrases that users enter into search engines that trigger the appearance of your ads.
  • Ads: Texts, images, or videos shown to users in search results.
  • Landing Page: Destination page where users are directed after clicking on an ad.

Campaign Setup

1. Create a Google Ads Account

To start, you need a Google Ads account. If you don't have one, follow these steps:

  1. Visit Google Ads.
  2. Click on "Get started now".
  3. Follow the instructions to create your account.

2. Define the Campaign Objective

The first step in setting up a campaign is to define its objective. Google Ads offers several options, such as:

  • Sales: Generate sales on your website.
  • Leads: Obtain contact information from potential customers.
  • Website Traffic: Increase the number of visits to your website.
  • Product and Brand Consideration: Increase interest in your products or services.
  • Brand Awareness and Reach: Increase the visibility of your brand.

3. Select the Campaign Type

Google Ads offers several types of campaigns. The most common are:

  • Search Network: Ads that appear in Google search results.
  • Display Network: Graphic ads that appear on websites associated with Google.
  • Shopping: Product ads that appear on Google Shopping.
  • Video: Ads on YouTube and other video sites.

4. Set the Budget and Bids

It is important to define how much you are willing to spend on your campaign. Google Ads allows you to set a daily budget and choose a bidding strategy. The most common options are:

  • Manual CPC: You decide how much to pay per click.
  • Automatic CPC: Google adjusts your bids to maximize clicks within your budget.
  • CPA (Cost per Acquisition): Pay for each conversion instead of per click.

5. Select Keywords

Keywords are fundamental for your ads to appear in relevant searches. To select the right keywords:

  1. Use the Google Ads Keyword Planner.
  2. Choose keywords that are relevant to your business.
  3. Consider the search volume and competition for each keyword.

6. Create the Ads

Ads should be attractive and relevant to users. A typical ad in the Search Network includes:

  • Headline: Text that appears in blue and is the link to the website.
  • Visible URL: Website address shown in the ad.
  • Description: Text that describes the product or service.

Ad example:

Headline: Buy Sports Shoes | Free Shipping on Orders > $50
Visible URL: www.example.com/sports-shoes
Description: Discover our collection of sports shoes. Buy now and get free shipping on orders over $50.

7. Set Up Targeting

Targeting allows your ads to reach the right people. You can target by:

  • Geographic Location: Country, city, or specific radius.
  • Language: Language spoken by users.
  • Devices: Computers, tablets, and mobiles.
  • Audiences: Groups of users with specific interests.

Conclusion

Setting up a basic SEM campaign in Google Ads involves several crucial steps, from creating the account to audience targeting. By following these steps, you can create effective campaigns that attract qualified traffic to your website and help you achieve your marketing goals. Always remember to monitor and adjust your campaigns to optimize their performance.
